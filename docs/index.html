How to Play Nim

1

The game starts with several heaps of stones.

2

On your turn, you can take away as many stones as you want from any one heap.

3

The player who removes the last stone from the board wins the game!

The Nim-Sum Strategy

First, switch each heap's chip count to binary (base 2), e.g. 110101 for 53 or 100101 for 37.

Compare the binary digits at each position (2^0, 2^1, etc.).
• 0 and 0 output 0
• 0 and 1 output 1
• 1 and 1 output 0

Example Case:

For pile sizes 1, 2, and 3:

1 -> 01
2 -> 10
3 -> 11
Nim-Sum -> 00

Since the result is 00, the player who starts from this position will lose against perfect play.

The goal is to move so that your opponent is left with a Nim-Sum of 0. To do this you can calculate the Nim-Sum of your position and figure out how many chips need to be subtracted to change it to 0. If you do this every turn, victory is guaranteed!
